## SECTION 1 — LEAD SCORING PHILOSOPHY

### 1.1 Why Lead Scoring Matters

In automotive retail, every salesperson manages between 30 and 150 active leads simultaneously. Without a structured scoring system, the human brain defaults to recency bias (calling whoever walked in today), familiarity bias (prioritising customers they like), and effort bias (avoiding hard conversations). The result is that genuinely ready buyers receive the same attention as idle browsers — and the dealership loses revenue that was already in the room.

Lead scoring solves this by converting qualitative impressions into a quantified, auditable, AI-verifiable signal. When a salesperson sees a score of 87/100 next to a customer's name, they immediately understand: this person deserves a callback within the next two hours, not tomorrow morning.

**The business case is overwhelming:**
- Dealerships that implement structured lead scoring close 28–35% more leads from the same prospect pool.
- The average automotive lead decays at a rate of 15% per day without meaningful follow-up.
- Studies across Indian automotive markets show that 60–70% of serious buyers who do not receive a response within 4 hours defect to a competitor.
- A scored lead pipeline reduces average time-to-close by 18–22%.

### 1.2 How Dealerships Lose Leads

Dealerships lose leads in predictable, preventable patterns:

**Speed failure.** A customer submits an online enquiry at 7:45 PM. No one calls until 10 AM the next morning. In those 14 hours, the customer has visited three competitor websites, test driven a Hyundai, and received a callback from a Kia salesperson who called within 22 minutes.

**Follow-up decay.** Salespeople make one or two follow-up calls and then stop. Industry data shows that 80% of automotive sales require 5 or more touchpoints, yet 78% of salespeople give up after two attempts.

**Misclassification.** A customer who says "I'm just looking" is classified as cold and deprioritised. In reality, "just looking" is often the socially comfortable way of saying "I'm serious but not yet ready to be pressured." Experienced salespeople know this. AI should too.

**Information vacuum.** When a salesperson leaves the dealership or changes teams, their lead knowledge walks out the door with them. No CRM entry, no notes, no handover. The next salesperson calls the customer as if it is their first contact, instantly destroying relationship capital.

**Uniform treatment.** All leads receive the same script, the same brochure, the same follow-up interval. A customer ready to buy today is treated identically to someone considering a purchase in six months. This frustrates buyers and dilutes urgency.

**The AI-powered lead intelligence platform is built to eliminate every one of these failure modes.**

### 1.3 How AI Should Evaluate Intent

AI intent evaluation is fundamentally different from human intuition. Human salespeople pattern-match on surface signals (appearance, confidence, vocabulary). AI must pattern-match on behavioural sequences, linguistic markers, temporal patterns, and cross-channel consistency.

**Behavioural sequence analysis.** A single test drive means curiosity. A test drive followed by a quotation request followed by an exchange value inquiry followed by a WhatsApp message asking about EMI options — that is a purchase trajectory. AI must treat the sequence, not the isolated event, as the primary signal.

**Linguistic intent markers.** When a customer shifts from "do you have the Creta?" to "when can I get the Creta in red?" — the grammatical shift from hypothetical to conditional to possessive is a direct linguistic indicator of purchase intent escalation. AI must be trained to identify these shifts.

**Temporal compression signals.** When the gap between touchpoints shrinks (first enquiry to second contact was 14 days; second to third was 3 days; now they're messaging twice in one day), the customer is self-accelerating toward a decision. This compression is one of the most reliable intent signals available.

**Cross-channel consistency.** A customer who visits the website, walks in, WhatsApps a question, and then calls to ask about registration costs is expressing consistent intent across four channels. Channel diversity multiplied by channel consistency is a strong buying signal.

**Dissonance detection.** When what a customer says ("I'm in no hurry") contradicts what they do (third visit this week, brought spouse along, asked about insurance), the AI must weight the behaviour over the statement.

### 1.4 Explicit vs. Implicit Buying Signals

**Explicit buying signals** are direct, unambiguous statements or actions that indicate purchase intent:
- "I want to book this car."
- "Can you give me the final on-road price?"
- "I have my cheque ready."
- Requesting a booking form.
- Asking about registration timeline.
- Asking which colour is available for immediate delivery.

These signals require zero inference. They are literal declarations of purchase intent and should immediately elevate the lead score by 15–25 points.

**Implicit buying signals** require contextual interpretation:
- Bringing a family member without being asked (implicit: this is a decision-making visit)
- Asking about seat covers and accessories (implicit: mentally decorating the car they intend to own)
- Asking about the service schedule for the first 3 years (implicit: planning long-term ownership)
- Lingering near a specific variant for 10+ minutes (implicit: emotional attachment forming)
- Asking what paperwork is needed (implicit: preparing to transact)
- Sending photos of the car to someone on WhatsApp while in the showroom (implicit: seeking external validation before committing)

Implicit signals carry 60–70% of the predictive value in most lead scoring models. Training AI to detect and weight implicit signals accurately is what separates a basic scoring tool from a genuine intelligence platform.

## SECTION 2 — CUSTOMER CLASSIFICATION FRAMEWORK

### Category 1: HOT+ (Score 85–100)

**Definition.** A customer with near-certain purchase intent, clear financial readiness, defined product preference, and an active, self-driven timeline. This customer is on the verge of booking. Every hour of delay in follow-up increases defection risk.

**Buying Probability:** 85–98%

**Typical Behaviours:**
- Has visited the showroom 2+ times
- Requested a final on-road price quotation
- Discussed financing or confirmed own funds
- Asked about delivery timeline for a specific variant/colour
- Has brought a spouse, parent, or key decision-maker to the showroom
- Has asked about registration and insurance process
- Has discussed trade-in / exchange value of existing vehicle
- Responds to WhatsApp and calls within minutes
- Has asked "what do I need to bring for booking?"

**Common Phrases:**
- "If I book today, when will I get delivery?"
- "What's the final amount I need to arrange?"
- "Can you hold this colour for me until tomorrow?"
- "My husband/wife has approved, we just need to finalise numbers."
- "I've decided on this variant, just checking a few things."
- "Will the offer be available until weekend?"
- "Is there anything else I need to sign today?"
- "I want to do the booking this week."
- "Can you send me the payment link?"
- "I've already checked insurance, just need your registration cost."

**Recommended Salesperson Actions:**
1. Call within 15 minutes of any new interaction.
2. Prepare a complete, personalised on-road price quote before calling.
3. Offer a specific booking slot ("I've kept Saturday 11 AM for you").
4. Remove every friction point — bring the forms, have the finance manager ready.
5. Do NOT introduce new options or variants (decision fatigue risk).
6. Confirm offer validity window without creating false pressure.
7. Send a WhatsApp summary with exact booking amount and next steps.
8. Loop in finance team if EMI is involved.
9. Escalate to sales manager if any hesitation is detected.
10. Follow up every 4–6 hours until booking confirmed.

**Follow-up Frequency:** Every 4–8 hours. No gap should exceed 24 hours.

### Category 2: HOT (Score 65–84)

**Definition.** A customer with strong purchase intent who has not yet committed. They are in active evaluation mode — comparing final options, resolving a specific concern, or waiting for an internal trigger (approval from family, salary credit, loan sanction). They need one or two more quality interactions to convert.

**Buying Probability:** 60–80%

**Typical Behaviours:**
- Has completed at least one test drive
- Is comparing 2–3 specific variants or models
- Has asked about EMI or financing options
- Has discussed timeline (next 2–4 weeks)
- Engages consistently on WhatsApp
- Has asked about exchange or trade-in value
- May be comparing with one competitor model

**Common Phrases:**
- "I'm almost decided, just need to confirm one thing."
- "Can you match the discount offered by [competitor]?"
- "I'll let you know by this weekend."
- "Can you send the detailed comparison between these two variants?"
- "My wife/husband wants to see it once before we finalise."
- "What's the best you can do on price?"
- "I need to check on the loan first."
- "The colour I want isn't in stock — when will it arrive?"

**Recommended Salesperson Actions:**
1. Call within 30 minutes of any engagement.
2. Identify and eliminate the one blocking concern.
3. Offer a test drive if not done yet.
4. Create mild urgency (limited stock, offer expiry, festival pricing).
5. Send a comparison document if they are evaluating variants.
6. Introduce finance manager if EMI is the concern.
7. Offer a home/office demo if they can't come to showroom.
8. Follow up every 24–36 hours.

**Follow-up Frequency:** Daily. Maximum gap of 36 hours.

### Category 3: WARM (Score 40–64)

**Definition.** A customer who has shown genuine interest but has not yet entered active decision mode. They are in research and consideration phase, comparing multiple options, possibly across brands. Their timeline is 1–3 months. They need consistent nurturing without pressure.

**Buying Probability:** 25–55%

**Typical Behaviours:**
- Has visited the showroom once or submitted an online enquiry
- Is exploring multiple brands and models
- Has asked general questions about features and pricing
- Has no confirmed timeline or is vague about it
- May have done a test drive but showed no urgency
- Engages occasionally but does not initiate contact

**Common Phrases:**
- "I'm still exploring my options."
- "I'll come back once I've checked a few more cars."
- "What's the starting price of this model?"
- "I'm not in a rush — maybe 2–3 months."
- "Can you send me a brochure?"
- "I'm comparing this with a Creta and a Seltos."
- "What's the mileage like in city traffic?"

**Recommended Salesperson Actions:**
1. Do not push for a booking conversation — build rapport and provide value.
2. Send useful content (comparison charts, video walkthroughs, mileage data).
3. Invite them to an upcoming event or product launch.
4. Check in with news updates (new colour, festival offer, EMI offer).
5. Maintain contact without being intrusive — once every 5–7 days.

**Follow-up Frequency:** Every 5–7 days. Weekly check-in.

### Category 4: COLD (Score 15–39)

**Definition.** A customer with weak or uncertain purchase intent. They may have expressed interest but show consistent disengagement, unclear requirements, significant financial barriers, or a timeline exceeding 6 months. They are not worth heavy salesperson investment but should remain in the nurture pipeline.

**Buying Probability:** 5–20%

**Typical Behaviours:**
- Does not respond to calls or WhatsApp
- Gave vague or inconsistent answers about budget and timeline
- Has not visited the showroom despite being invited
- Says they are looking "next year" or "after some event"
- Price-shops aggressively without any real intent signals
- Engages only when offers or discounts are mentioned

**Common Phrases:**
- "I'll think about it."
- "I'll call you when I'm ready."
- "I'm looking for next Diwali."
- "Just tell me the price, I'll see."
- "Why is it so expensive? The other brand is cheaper."
- "Send me all the details on WhatsApp, I'll go through it."

**Recommended Salesperson Actions:**
1. Move to automated nurture sequence — no manual daily follow-ups.
2. Send one valuable touchpoint per week (offer, news, video).
3. Re-qualify after 30 days with a single outreach.
4. Do not allocate prime salesperson time.

**Follow-up Frequency:** Once every 10–14 days. Automated where possible.

### Category 5: DEAD LEAD (Score 0–14)

**Definition.** A lead that has either explicitly ruled out a purchase, is definitively unreachable, has booked with a competitor and confirmed delivery, or provides fraudulent contact information. Continuing to allocate sales resources to dead leads is actively harmful — it consumes time that should go to live opportunities.

**Buying Probability:** 0–4%

**Typical Behaviours:**
- Confirmed booking with competitor
- Phone number does not exist or is switched off permanently
- Email bounces
- Gave a fake name and number at showroom
- Explicitly said "I am not interested"
- Timeline is beyond 18 months with no other signals
- Responded abusively or asked to be removed from contact list

**Common Phrases:**
- "I already bought a [competitor brand]."
- "Please stop calling me."
- "I changed my mind, not buying any car."
- "I was just checking for a friend."
- [No response after 8+ attempts across 30+ days]
- "I'll never be able to afford this."

**Recommended Salesperson Actions:**
1. Mark as Dead Lead in CRM with reason code.
2. Remove from active follow-up queue immediately.
3. Archive with full interaction history for ML training.
4. Set a 6-month dormant re-activation trigger (circumstances change).
5. Do not invest any manual follow-up time.

**Follow-up Frequency:** None. Single re-activation attempt at 6-month mark if circumstances may have changed.

## SECTION 3 — SCORING MODEL (100-POINT FRAMEWORK)

### Overview

The total lead score is computed across eight weighted dimensions. Each dimension contributes a maximum number of points proportional to its predictive value in automotive purchase conversion. The score is computed fresh at each CRM interaction, with a timestamp and audit log.

| Dimension | Max Points | Weight |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Intent Score | 25 | 25% |
| Engagement Score | 20 | 20% |
| Urgency Score | 15 | 15% |
| Financial Readiness | 15 | 15% |
| Product Fit | 10 | 10% |
| Competitive Risk | 5 | 5% |
| Relationship Strength | 5 | 5% |
| Sentiment Score | 5 | 5% |
| **TOTAL** | **100** | **100%** |

---

### 3.1 Intent Score (Max 25 Points)

**Description.** Measures the directness and strength of purchase intent based on customer statements, actions, and inquiry specificity.

**Weight:** 25 points (highest weight — the most direct predictor of sale conversion)

**Positive Indicators:**
- Asked for final on-road price quotation: +8
- Confirmed variant and colour choice: +7
- Asked about booking process or required documents: +7
- Asked about delivery timeline for specific unit: +6
- Stated a personal purchase deadline: +5
- Asked about registration and insurance: +4
- Multiple specific feature questions about one variant: +3

**Negative Indicators:**
- General browsing questions with no specificity: -3
- Asking for prices "for reference only": -4
- Says "just exploring, no hurry": -5
- Asked about a model that doesn't fit their stated need: -2
- Contradictory variant preferences across interactions: -4

**Edge Cases:**
- Customer asks detailed questions but claims no interest: weight behaviour +3, statement -0 (behaviour dominates)
- Customer is buying for a family member: score intent of the buyer, not the end user
- Corporate enquiry: adjust intent scoring to procurement decision tree, not individual signals

**Examples:**
- Customer asks "What's the on-road price of the top variant Creta in Chennai?" → Intent: +8
- Customer asks "What cars do you have under 10 lakhs?" → Intent: +1
- Customer says "I want to book before March 31 for tax purposes" → Intent: +5 urgency bonus

---

### 3.2 Engagement Score (Max 20 Points)

**Description.** Measures the depth, frequency, and consistency of customer interaction across all channels.

**Weight:** 20 points

**Positive Indicators:**
- Walk-in visit: +5 per visit (max 2 counted = +10)
- Completed test drive: +6
- Responded to WhatsApp within 1 hour: +3
- Opened and replied to email within 24 hours: +2
- Attended showroom event: +4
- Website visit with 3+ page views: +2
- Called dealership proactively: +4
- Responded within same day: +2

**Negative Indicators:**
- No response to 3+ consecutive outreach attempts: -5
- Opened WhatsApp messages but did not reply (blue tick, no response): -2
- Missed scheduled appointment without notice: -3
- Engagement dropped significantly after initial contact: -4

**Edge Cases:**
- Customer is very busy (surgeon, executive) — lower engagement does not mean low intent; check other dimensions
- WhatsApp engagement spikes only when offers are sent — indicates price sensitivity, not genuine intent

**Examples:**
- Two walk-ins + completed test drive + same-day WhatsApp reply = 10+6+3 = 19/20
- One enquiry form submission + no response in 2 weeks = 2 - 5 = -3 (floor at 0)

---

### 3.3 Urgency Score (Max 15 Points)

**Description.** Measures time pressure signals — both stated and inferred.

**Weight:** 15 points

**Positive Indicators:**
- Confirmed purchase deadline (specific date): +10
- Festival, tax, or financial year-end deadline mentioned: +8
- Told another salesperson they are booking this week: +7
- Competitor offer expiry mentioned: +6
- Vehicle urgently needed (marriage, travel, job start): +9
- Mentioned current vehicle has broken down: +8
- Asked about fastest available delivery: +5

**Negative Indicators:**
- "No hurry, will decide when I'm ready": -6
- Timeline beyond 6 months: -8
- Waiting for unconfirmed future event (bonus, promotion): -4
- Has been "almost ready" for 60+ days: -5

**Edge Cases:**
- Customer mentions urgency but has outstanding financial issue — flag as high urgency, low financial readiness; resolve finance first
- Urgency is artificially created by salesperson during follow-up — AI must validate urgency source

---

### 3.4 Financial Readiness (Max 15 Points)

**Description.** Measures ability and readiness to fund the purchase.

**Weight:** 15 points

**Positive Indicators:**
- Confirmed own funds (cash/savings): +15
- Loan pre-approved: +13
- Cheque or DD ready: +14
- Salary certificate and employment proof submitted: +8
- Budget aligns with desired variant: +5
- Asked detailed EMI calculation for specific amount: +6
- Previously purchased a car (demonstrated financial history): +4

**Negative Indicators:**
- Budget significantly lower than desired variant: -8
- Loan rejected by bank: -10
- No clarity on budget at all: -5
- Waiting for bonus (uncertain date): -4
- Self-employed with irregular income: -2

---

### 3.5 Product Fit (Max 10 Points)

**Description.** Measures alignment between customer's stated needs and available inventory.

**Weight:** 10 points

**Positive Indicators:**
- Desired variant in stock: +10
- Customer needs match product features (family size, use case, budget): +7
- Customer has test driven and expressed positive feedback: +6
- Dealer has exact colour and variant in stock: +8

**Negative Indicators:**
- Desired variant has 8+ week wait: -4
- Significant feature gap between expectation and product: -5
- Customer comparing model that outperforms your offering: -3

---

### 3.6 Competitive Risk (Max 5 Points — Penalty Model)

**Description.** Measures defection risk to competitor brands and models.

**Weight:** 5 points (penalty-based — starts at 5, deductions applied)

- No competitor comparison mentioned: 5/5
- Comparing with one competitor: -1
- Actively test drove competitor: -2
- Received competitor quotation: -2
- Has competitor model as first choice, yours as backup: -4
- Already booked competitor: -5 (scores 0)

---

### 3.7 Relationship Strength (Max 5 Points)

**Description.** Measures the quality of the human relationship between customer and salesperson/dealership.

**Weight:** 5 points

- Existing customer (repeat buyer): +5
- Referred by satisfied customer: +4
- Has had positive service experience: +3
- Knows salesperson personally: +3
- New customer, first interaction: +1

---

### 3.8 Sentiment Score (Max 5 Points)

**Description.** NLP-based measurement of emotional tone, positivity, and enthusiasm in customer communication.

**Weight:** 5 points

- Consistently positive, enthusiastic language: +5
- Neutral, transactional tone: +3
- Cautious or skeptical tone: +2
- Negative language, complaint history: +1
- Hostile or abusive communication: 0
